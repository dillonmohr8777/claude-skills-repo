# State Advertising Rules — how to look them up

Cannabis advertising law is **state-by-state** and changes often. Never quote a specific rule from memory — look it up at the state regulator, note the date, and default conservative.

## How to research a state (do this per client)

1. Identify the state's cannabis regulator (e.g. CA DCC, CO MED, MI CRA, NY OCM, IL IDFPR, MA CCC).
2. Find its **advertising & marketing regulations** section (usually in the administrative code).
3. Extract: audience-composition threshold, required warnings/disclaimers (exact text), prohibited content, sign/billboard rules, proximity-to-schools rules, influencer/endorsement rules, and any pre-approval requirement.
4. Cross-check the **platform** policy (see advertising-playbook.md) and **FTC/FDA** claim rules.
5. Record source URL + date in the deliverable.

## Common threads across most legal states

- **Audience composition:** ads only where a defined majority (frequently **≥71.6%**) of the audience is reasonably expected to be 21+.
- **No youth appeal:** no cartoons, toys, characters, candy imagery.
- **No health/medical/efficacy claims** without substantiation (and generally not at all for structure/function disease claims).
- **Mandatory warnings** (age, keep from children, impairment, "not FDA-evaluated" for CBD).
- **No unsubstantiated superlatives** ("strongest," "safest," "best").
- **Location limits:** billboards near schools/parks/highways restricted or banned.
- **Age-gate** for any digital property.
- **Opt-in** for direct marketing (email/SMS).

## Product-class scrutiny (high → low)

Delta-8 / THCA / novel cannabinoids  >  marijuana/THC  >  ingestible CBD  >  topical/non-ingestible CBD  >  ancillary/B2B (software, accessories). Match creative freedom to the class — accessories and software have near-normal latitude.

## Legality status changes

Adult-use vs. medical-only vs. CBD-only vs. illegal shifts election-to-election. Verify the client's specific states each engagement; don't assume last year's map. Geo-fence to currently-legal states only.

> Bottom line: this file tells you *how to find* the rule, not *what it is* for a given state today. Always verify live and flag anything license-risky for the client's counsel.
