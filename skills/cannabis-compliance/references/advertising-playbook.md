# Cannabis Advertising Playbook — how to actually scale

Mainstream paid social is mostly closed to cannabis. Scale comes from owned channels + endemic networks + compliant organic. Set this expectation with the client on day one so nobody promises "just run Meta ads."

## The realistic channel stack (highest ROI first)

1. **SEO + local SEO** — Dispensaries win on "dispensary near me", strain/product pages, and Google Business Profile. Free, compounding, no platform ban. Invest here first.
2. **Email + SMS (owned, opt-in, 21+)** — Your safest scalable paid-equivalent. TCPA-compliant opt-in, easy unsubscribe, no health claims. Loyalty/rewards drive repeat.
3. **Cannabis-endemic programmatic** — Surfside, Fyllo, Mantis, traffic on Leafly/Weedmaps. Built for compliance (geo/age built in). This is your "paid scale" answer.
4. **Weedmaps / Leafly listings** — Category-specific discovery; effectively the "Google" of dispensary shopping.
5. **Organic social (brand, not paid)** — IG/TikTok for brand/education/lifestyle. Avoid depicting consumption/sales; expect occasional takedowns; keep a backup/owned audience. Link-in-bio to age-gated site.
6. **Influencer / creator** — Works well; MUST use FTC disclosure (`#ad`, `#sponsored`, clear + conspicuous), 21+ creators, no health claims, no youth audience.
7. **Out-of-home / print / radio / podcast** — Allowed in many states subject to **audience composition** rules (commonly ≥71.6% of audience reasonably expected to be 21+).
8. **PR / earned + events** — Underrated for brand.

## Platform paid-ad status (verify before spend — 2026 baseline)

- **Meta (FB/IG):** No paid cannabis/THC ads. CBD extremely limited/effectively no. Use organic + owned.
- **Google Ads:** THC largely banned; **CBD possible with LegitScript certification** (COAs, compliant site, ~2–4 wk approval, annual fee). Limited dispensary/non-ingestible pilots in some geos.
- **TikTok:** Paid banned; organic risky.
- **X/Twitter:** Most permissive major platform — requires pre-authorization + geo/age targeting + certified advertiser status.
- **Reddit / Amazon / LinkedIn:** Cannabis paid generally restricted; ancillary/B2B sometimes OK.

## Copy compliance quick-filter (every asset)

Cut or rewrite if it: makes a health/medical/efficacy claim; could appeal to minors; shows over-consumption/driving/public use; omits required age + state warnings; is served where illegal; uses unsubstantiated superlatives ("strongest," "cures," "#1," "safest").

## FTC influencer rules (non-negotiable)

- Clear + conspicuous disclosure (`#ad` / `#sponsored`) — not buried in hashtags.
- No claims the brand couldn't legally make itself (health claims transfer liability).
- Creator must be 21+ and audience predominantly 21+.
- Keep signed disclosure agreements on file.

## KPIs that survive the constraints

First-party list growth, email/SMS revenue, organic + local search rank, Weedmaps/Leafly conversion, LTV/repeat rate, endemic-programmatic ROAS. De-emphasize channels you can't legally buy.
