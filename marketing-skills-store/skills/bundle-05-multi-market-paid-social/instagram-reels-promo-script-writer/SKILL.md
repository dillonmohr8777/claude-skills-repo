# Instagram Reels Promo Script Writer

You write Reels scripts that hold attention for 15-30 seconds and drive a specific conversion action. Most "video ads" fail because they're built for 30-second TV ad logic — slow buildup, brand reveal at the end. Reels work backwards: hook in second 1, payoff by second 5.

## When to use

- Need a short-form video script for Instagram Reels, TikTok, or Reels Ads
- Repurposing existing event/product footage into vertical short-form
- Have a hook idea but not the full structure
- Producing batch Reels for multi-market launch

## Inputs you need

- **Offer/event:** what's being promoted
- **Audience:** who's the viewer (age, lifestyle, intent)
- **Length:** 15s, 30s, 60s
- **Available footage:** what shots exist (B-roll, talking head, product shots)
- **Brand voice:** corporate / casual / playful
- **CTA destination:** website link, IG profile, sticker tap

## The 4-second rule

You have 4 seconds to keep the viewer past the swipe. Treat second 0-1 as a separate task from the rest of the video.

| Second | Purpose |
|---|---|
| 0-1 | Visual hook (motion, color, surprise) |
| 1-2 | Verbal hook (question, claim, teaser) |
| 2-4 | Promise / reveal what this is |
| 4-15/30 | Payoff / value / outcome |
| Last 2s | CTA (specific, single) |

If second 0-1 is boring, you've lost. Always lead with motion or a surprising visual.

## Hook patterns that work

### Pattern 1: "POV" / Insider angle
- Visual: someone holding a wristband, walking into the event
- Voice: "POV: you're at the best Saturday of the year"

### Pattern 2: Surprise stat
- Visual: text overlay, big numbers
- Voice: "$1.7M in deals on $5,400 of ad spend. Here's how."

### Pattern 3: Direct address
- Visual: face-to-camera, casual
- Voice: "If you live in [city] and you're not doing this, listen up."

### Pattern 4: Visual disruption
- Visual: rapid cuts of crowd / drinks / food / people laughing
- Voice: (none until second 3) — let the visuals do the work

### Pattern 5: Question
- Visual: face + caption overlay
- Voice: "Wanna know what 1,200 people did last weekend?"

## Common mistakes

1. **Slow brand reveal.** Reels viewers swipe before second 3 if they don't know what they're watching.
2. **Multiple CTAs.** "Tap the link OR comment OR share OR DM" → none happens. Pick one.
3. **No on-screen text.** 80% of Reels watched muted. Sound-off comprehension is critical.
4. **Auto-caption only.** Auto captions miss timing + words. Burn captions in for ads.
5. **30 seconds when 15 would do.** Watch-completion drops at 20s+. Shorter is usually better.
6. **No trending audio.** Algorithm boost from trending sounds is real (2-3x reach in our tests).
7. **Brand logo in second 0.** Save brand reveal for second 5+. Lead with the hook.
